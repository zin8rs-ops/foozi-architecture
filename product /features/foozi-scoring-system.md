# Foozi Scoring System

The Foozi Scoring System converts complex football data into simple, interpretable scores.

The goal is to provide users with quick signals that summarize predictions, team strength and player performance.

---

# Core Scores

The platform uses several scoring models.

Tactra Score  
Confidence Score  
Team Strength Index  
Player Impact Rating  
Momentum Score  

These scores appear across match pages, competition pages, team pages and player pages.

---

# 1 Tactra Score

The Tactra Score represents the predicted outcome probability for a match.

Example

Bayern vs Dortmund

Home Win — 64%  
Draw — 21%  
Away Win — 15%

The score is derived from the Tactra Prediction Engine.

Signals include

recent form  
expected goals  
home advantage  
squad strength  
head-to-head history

The score should always be visualized with probability bars.

---

# 2 Confidence Score

The Confidence Score represents how reliable a prediction is.

Example

Prediction Confidence: 82%

Signals include

data consistency  
team stability  
historical predictability  
injury uncertainty

Higher confidence means the model has stronger supporting signals.

---

# 3 Team Strength Index

The Team Strength Index estimates the overall strength of a team.

Range

0 – 100

Example

Bayern Munich — 91  
Borussia Dortmund — 84

Signals include

goal difference  
expected goals  
recent results  
squad value

This score allows comparison across competitions.

---

# 4 Player Impact Rating

The Player Impact Rating measures how strongly a player influences matches.

Example

Erling Haaland — 9.2  
Jamal Musiala — 8.7

Signals include

goals  
assists  
expected goals contribution  
chances created

This score is displayed on player pages and team pages.

---

# 5 Momentum Score

The Momentum Score reflects current form and performance trends.

Example

Arsenal Momentum Score — 88

Signals include

recent wins  
goal streaks  
defensive stability

Momentum helps highlight teams currently in strong form.

---

# Score Visualization

Scores should always be displayed visually.

Examples

probability bars  
score badges  
trend indicators  

Clear visual signals help users interpret data quickly.

---
